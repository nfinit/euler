TI BASIC solutions for the Texas Instruments Compact Computer 40
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Solutions in this directory are implemented in the version of [TI BASIC]
specific to Texas Instruments' [Compact Computer 40], a miniscule portable
8-bit computer developed and released by TI in 1983. Although the CC40 has
little in common with the [TI-99/4] family of home computers from an
architectural standpoint, its BASIC environment is mostly similar to TI's
Extended BASIC, albeit lacking in graphics and sound functionality due to the
CC40's lack of either. Unlike BASIC on the TI-99/4, the CC40's interpreter is
also written in assembly, rather than the infamously glacial [GPL].

While the CC40 is capable of running TI BASIC software and supports most of
the same core functionality, CC40 BASIC solutions are considered separate from
[TI-99/4 solutions] mostly due to the need to accomodate for its single-line 31 
character LCD display.
